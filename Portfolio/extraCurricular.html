Extra Curricular Activities

  • As a member of the Institute of Physics I have attended many lectures over the past year during my time at university. Becoming involved with physics outside of my degree, it’s not just a career, it’s a passion.

  • Vice President & Social Secretary of the Astronomy and Physics Society at NTU for 2 years. Running the society and its events, by scheduling, budgeting and negotiating with the student union. It involves pushing for memberships, and organising trips abroad.

  • During my time at Eaton Bank Academy, I was elected deputy Head Boy and then Head Boy in my final year. Representing the school in the wider community, I mentored students and met with parents and teachers. My role involved public speaking at school open evenings in front of prospective students and their parents.

  • Black belt in Ju-Jitsu, showing determination and hard work to reach this level by the age of 16.  I also taught juniors learning Ju-Jitsu, gave demonstrations and showed what skills were needed to gain their next grading.

  • Being a member of the local Youth Council, getting the young voices heard and being involved with all the town’s important activities. As part of this, I helped deliver a letter to our local MP at the Houses of Parliament, making sure they knew what the young people of the area were hoping the council could make progress on. 

  • Travel is one of my favourite pastimes. I am very passionate about visiting new countries and experiencing new cultures. I have visited opposite ends of the spectrum regarding wealth and cultures and every time I learn and gain personally from these experiences. I’ve travelled on my own many times, showing organisation and commitment of my own accord.
